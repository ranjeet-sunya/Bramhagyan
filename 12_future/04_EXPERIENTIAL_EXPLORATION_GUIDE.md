## 1. NIRVIKALPA SAMADHI EXPLORATION

### Different Interpretations:

**Interpretation A (Advaita):**
- Observer (Sakshi) is ALWAYS present
- In Nirvikalpa, observer has nothing left to observe
- But observer doesn't "dissolve" â€” just rests in itself
- Like eye that cannot see itself

**Interpretation B (Kashmir Shaivism):**
- Nirvikalpa = Shiva-Shakti union
- Observer and observed merge
- Not dissolution but RECOGNITION of non-separation
- Observer realizes it was always the observed

**Interpretation C (Buddhist Jhana):**
- Progressive dissolution of mental factors
- 8th Jhana = Neither perception nor non-perception
- Beyond that = Cessation (Nirodha)
- Consciousness stops, then restarts

**Interpretation D (Yoga Sutras):**
- Asamprajnata Samadhi = Without seed
- Only Samskaras remain
- Eventually even those dissolve
- Kaivalya = Pure isolation of consciousness

### Steps to Try:

```

1. Sit comfortably, close eyes
2. Watch thoughts arise and pass
3. Notice: "Who is watching the thoughts?"
4. Turn attention to the WATCHER itself

EXPERIMENT 2: NETI NETI TO THE END

EXPERIMENT 3: SLEEP-WAKE TRANSITION
â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€â”€
```

## 2. SUBTLE BODY (PRANAMAYA KOSHA) OBSERVATION

### Different Interpretations:

**Interpretation A (Vedantic):**
- Pranamaya = Layer of vital energy
- Distinct from physical but interpenetrating
- Can be perceived through refined attention

**Interpretation B (Yogic):**
- Pranamaya = 72,000 Nadis
- 5 Pranas + 5 Upa-Pranas
- Perceivable as flows, currents, sensations

**Interpretation C (Tantric):**
- Subtle body = Energy body
- Chakras as vortexes
- Can be seen with Divya Drishti (divine sight)

### Steps to Try:

```
2. Scan body from toes to head
```
